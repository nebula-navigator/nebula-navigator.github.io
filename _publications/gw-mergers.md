**Abstract:**

Stellar-mass binary black holes (sBBHs) formed in globular clusters (GCs) are promising sources for multiband gravitational wave (GW) observations, particularly with low- and middle-frequency detectors. These sBBHs can retain detectable eccentricities as they enter the sensitivity bands of space- and ground-based GW observatories. Using Monte Carlo simulations of escaping sBBHs from GC models, we assess the detectability and parameter estimation precision across combinations of detectors including LISA, Taiji, the LISA-Taiji network, and AMIGO. Results indicate that future multiband networks can detect and constrain eccentricities with high precision, offering unique insights into binary black hole formation pathways. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
